# Image Sources for Disaster Articles

This document lists the recommended images for each disaster, all from Wikimedia Commons (Creative Commons licensed or Public Domain).

## How to Download

1. Visit the Wikimedia Commons category URL
2. Click on the recommended image
3. Click "Download" or right-click "Original file" and save
4. Rename to `hero.jpg` and place in the correct directory

## General Guidelines

### Image Criteria:
- ✅ Wikimedia Commons (CC-licensed or Public Domain)
- ✅ High resolution (at least 1920px wide for hero images)
- ✅ Respectful (avoid graphic content)
- ✅ Historically accurate
- ✅ Clear and well-composed

### What to Avoid:
- ❌ Graphic images of victims or severe injuries
- ❌ Copyrighted news agency photos
- ❌ Low resolution images
- ❌ Watermarked images

### Licensing:
All images from Wikimedia Commons are:
- Creative Commons licensed (CC BY-SA 2.0, CC BY 3.0, etc.)
- Public Domain (CC0, PD-US-Gov, etc.)

Remember to attribute sources if required by the license.

## Quick Download Steps

For each disaster:

1. Visit the Wikimedia Commons category URL above
2. Browse available images
3. Click on your chosen image
4. On the image page, click "More details" or scroll to file information
5. Right-click "Original file" and "Save Image As..."
6. Rename to `hero.jpg`
7. Move to `/public/images/disasters/[slug]/hero.jpg`

If you want to proceed without downloading images immediately, you can create simple placeholder images or use generic aircraft type photos as temporary placeholders.
